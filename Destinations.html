Tourism in New Zealand

Destinations

Coromandel Peninsula

Coromandel Peninsula is an area located in the north Island of New zealand. It is New Zealand's most popular summer holiday destination. Coromandel is known for its pristine beaches, secluded bays and misty forest. The Coromandel offers hundreds of hideaways and activities, making it an ideal place to escape. Also located in Coromandel, is the well known Cathedral Cove, an archway rock formation. Coromandel Peninsula is also home to Hot Water Beach, a beach where holes can be dug to reveal hot water under the sand, allowing you to relax in warm water while looking out into the ocean right in front of you.

Auckland

Auckland is New Zealand's largest city. Auckland is based around 2 large harbors. Auckland has so much to offer, making it a popular tourist destination. In the center you have the high rises, surrounding the iconic sky tower, with stunning 360 views. At night you can go into the city, as you have access to several bars and restaurants, within walking distances. If you don't want to stay within the city center, there are many other suburbs in Auckland to stay, also offering fun tourist activities.

Rotorua

Rotorua is a city in the Bay of Plenty of the north island. It is known for its Maori Culture, geothermal activity, and its many lakes. Rotorua has many beautiful natural wonders to visit such as mud pools, natural hot springs, forest and lakes. It also has many other family friendly activities from skyline and agrodome to culture tours and performances. It also had high adrenaline activities such as white water rafting, ziplines and mountain biking.

Wellington

Wellington is New Zealand's capital city, found at the bottom of the north island. Wellington is famous for a vibrant creative culture and is known as the culinary capital, having tucked away bars, quirky cafes and award winning restaurants. Wellington has activities such as museums, art galleries and theater shows, making it a great place to visit. Wellington is also the end point of the north Island before you cross Cook strait to get to the south island. From Wellington you can take a 31/2 hour ferry to Picton, in the south island.

Nelson

Nelson is a city in the south island, facing Tasman Bay. Nelson is the sunniest place in New Zealand, where you can enjoy arts and crafts, great creative culture and beautiful beaches. It is also known for its locally grown products and freshly caught seafood. Nelson is also a base for visiting Abel Tasman. Able Tasman is known for its golden sand beaches, beautiful reserves and national parks offering excellent walking and tramping activities.

Queenstown

Queenstown, New Zealand, sits on the South Island’s Lake Wakatipu and set against the Southern Alps. Queenston is one of New Zealand’s most popular tourist destinations and is known as the adventure capital of the world, offering a range of tourist activities. Queenston is good for ones who seek an adrenaline adventure, with skydiving, bungy jumping, rafting, and ziplining. Queenston is unique in how it attracts tourism all year round. In winter Queenston is known for winter, such as skiing and snowboarding. Even if you want a more chill holiday, you can cruise Lake Wakatipu, soak in the Hot pools, go on a wine tour, ride the gondola or just soak in the beautiful nature.
